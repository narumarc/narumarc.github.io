About Me

I am an Applied Data Scientist and Data Engineer with a background in theoretical and computational physics. I was trained in high-energy physics, where I worked on Monte-Carlo simulations, statistical model validation, and large-scale numerical experiments for particle interactions.

This experience shaped the way I work with data: I treat models as scientific objects that must be understood, tested, and validated ‚Äî not just optimized. I focus on uncertainty, robustness, and whether a system can be trusted in real-world conditions.

Today, I build end-to-end data and machine learning systems: scalable cloud pipelines, predictive and prescriptive models, automated decision tools, and production-ready ML platforms with monitoring and analytics. My work bridges data engineering, machine learning, and scientific modeling.
